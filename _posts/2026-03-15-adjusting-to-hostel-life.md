Starting hostel life was one of the biggest changes in my university journey.

Before university, I had never lived away from home. Suddenly, I had to manage my time, studies, routine, and personal responsibilities on my own. In the beginning, this felt uncomfortable and stressful.

There were small things that became big challenges, like maintaining a study schedule, managing sleep, and staying focused in a new environment. At times I missed home a lot, and it affected my motivation.

With time, I started to understand that adjustment does not happen in one day. It happens through patience and repeated effort. I began to create a better routine for myself and slowly became more independent.

## What Hostel Life Taught Me

- How to manage my time better
- How to continue studying without perfect conditions
- How to become more responsible
- How to stay patient during difficult changes

## Looking Forward

Hostel life was difficult at first, but it also made me stronger. It taught me self-discipline, and that lesson is helping me both in my studies and in my personal life.
