My first semester in Computer Engineering was challenging, but it became an important part of my growth.

I started learning Python and basic machine learning ideas without having a strong programming background. At first, even simple concepts felt difficult, and I often needed more time than others to understand programming logic.

Instead of giving up, I kept practicing. Little by little, I began to understand more, and that gave me confidence to continue.

I also faced personal challenges during this time. I had hoped for admission in Lahore, but I came to Faisalabad instead. Hostel life was completely new for me, and living away from home was not easy in the beginning.

My GPA was below 3, and that disappointed me. Still, I decided to treat that result as a lesson instead of a stopping point.

## Key Learnings

- Learned Python from the beginning
- Built a basic understanding of machine learning
- Improved problem-solving through practice
- Realized that consistency matters more than fast results

## Challenges

> Weak programming background
>
> Difficulty in understanding new concepts
>
> Adjusting to hostel life
>
> Managing disappointment after a low GPA

## My Plan

- Improve my GPA in the next semester
- Practice coding every day
- Build small projects for experience
- Stay disciplined and focused

## Conclusion

The first semester built my foundation. It was not easy, but it made me more serious, more focused, and more ready for the journey ahead.
