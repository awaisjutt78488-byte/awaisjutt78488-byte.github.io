Before university, I did not have a proper programming background.

I had only a little exposure to the C language in 12th class, and even that was not enough to give me strong confidence in programming. Because of this, learning Python at university felt much more challenging.

Many basic concepts that others already understood were new for me. I had to spend more time understanding logic, syntax, and the way programs work.

Even though this made my journey harder, it also taught me patience. I realized that a weak beginning does not mean a weak future.

## My Situation at the Start

- No strong programming base
- Only a little exposure to C language
- Needed extra effort to catch up

## What I Believe Now

Anyone can improve with practice, even if the start is weak.
